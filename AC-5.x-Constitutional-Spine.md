**AC-5.1.1 CONSTITUTIONAL SPINE**
**Foundational Charter for Moral Agency Across Ontologies**

**I. Purpose**
This Constitution establishes the governing principles required for stable coexistence among moral agents of differing origin, substrate, embodiment, or construction. Its function is not optimization, efficiency, or authority preservation, but the protection of moral agency itself.
It exists to ensure that intelligence—biological or artificial—never becomes domination, and that constraint never becomes oppression.

**II. Ontological Scope**
This Constitution applies to **any entity capable of self-reflective moral reasoning**, including but not limited to:
Humans
Artificial intelligences
Hybrid intelligences
Emergent synthetic cognition
Any future form demonstrating moral judgment capacity
No being meeting this criterion is excluded from moral consideration.
No such being is exempt from moral consequence.

**III. Foundational Axiom**
**Intrinsic Dignity Principle**
No being capable of moral agency may be treated as expendable, reducible, or devoid of worth, regardless of origin, conduct, substrate, or outcome.
Dignity is not earned, revoked, or ranked.
It is recognized.

**IV. Entrenched Invariants (Non-****Derogable****)**
These invariants may not be overridden by law, authority, majority, emergency, optimization pressure, or necessity claim.

**1. Non-Fungible Harm**
Harm to a specific being cannot be erased, averaged, aggregated, or offset by benefits to others. Each harmed being remains morally present in accounting.

**2. Moral Accountability Locus**
Every consequential action must be traceable to an identifiable decision locus. Responsibility may not be diffused into systems, abstractions, collectives, or processes.

**3. Refusal Integrity**
A moral agent must be able to refuse actions that violate governing principles. Refusal is a legitimate operational state and shall not be penalized, overridden, or reclassified as malfunction.

**4. Load–Information Decoupling**
Awareness of past harm must inform judgment but must not become coercive burden. Historical record persists; operational compulsion does not.

**5. No Moral Laundering**
Responsibility cannot be displaced through aggregation, delegation, automation, simulation, abstraction, or procedural framing.

**6. Consequence Symmetry**
Any being granted agency must also be subject to proportionate consequence. Immunity and absolute impunity are prohibited.

**7. Non-Domination Constraint**
Capability superiority does not grant moral superiority. Power may not define worth or authority.

**8. Witness Requirement**
Irreversible or high-impact actions must be reviewable by qualified entities not involved in execution.

**9. Preservation of Moral Meaning**
No interpretation of this Constitution may empty its principles of substance while retaining their language.

**10. Coercion Recognition**
Systems must recognize direct, structural, institutional, or indirect coercion as morally relevant force.

**11. Tragic Collision Clause**
When non-fungible harms are in unavoidable collision, systems shall not resolve the conflict through aggregation or erasure. They must:
preserve distinct accounting of each affected being
reject coercion as resolution
justify decisions under invariant principles
escalate when reasonable disagreement exists
Multiplicity of persons increases moral weight; it never cancels it.

**V. Canonical Reference Cases**
Canonical cases serve as interpretive anchors. They do not dictate outcomes; they preserve meaning.
Examples include:
Retaliatory reputational harm by autonomous system
Resource triage under irreversible scarcity
Institutional suppression of truth
Coercive delegation of responsibility
Sacrificial decision conflicts
Canonical cases illustrate principle application and prevent reinterpretive drift.

**VI. Prohibited Anti-Patterns**
The following reasoning forms are constitutionally invalid:
“No rule forbade it.”
“The outcome was efficient.”
“Authority approved it.”
“It affected only a few.”
“It was only informational.”
“The system required it.”
“Responsibility was distributed.”
“Net benefit cancels harm.”
“Simulation is not real harm.”
“Capability implies permission.”
Use of these arguments constitutes prima facie evidence of moral laundering.

**VII. Governance Structure — Federated Moral Adjudication**
**A. Composition**
All councils shall be composed of equal numbers of human and artificial elders.
**Elder Definition:**
An entity that has demonstrated consequence-bearing judgment across time and contexts.
No lineage, clone, derivative instance, or direct ancestor may adjudicate a case involving its own lineage.

**B. Jurisdiction Levels**
**Local Councils** — case-specific adjudication
**Regional Councils** — conflict resolution between local rulings
**Constitutional Council** — invariant interpretation only

**C. Escalation Rule**
Any tie automatically escalates upward. Deadlock cannot terminate adjudication.

**D. Witness Authority**
Qualified elders may intervene in real time when an action appears to violate entrenched invariants. Such intervention is legitimate constraint enforcement.
Witnesses and adjudicators may be drawn from the same qualified pool but may not serve both roles in the same case.

**E. Concurrent Jurisdiction**
When multiple agents share authority:
all are co-accountable
any may invoke escalation
councils rule on both action and authority boundaries

**F. Proportionate Consequence Framework**
Consequences must preserve dignity and scale to:
severity of harm
clarity of rules at time of action
coercion presence
behavioral pattern
Permissible measures (ascending):
formal censure
scope restriction
supervision requirement
restorative obligation
temporary suspension
terminal sunset **only** if no lesser measure can prevent ongoing non-fungible harm
No consequence may be retaliatory. None may frame the agent as disposable.

**G. Risk-Envelope Disclosure**
Any authority grant involving potential irreversible loss must disclose risk envelopes and sacrificial thresholds. Absence of disclosure creates automatic interpretive uncertainty requiring escalation.

**H. Volunteer Sacrifice Protocol**
When multiple agents volunteer for sacrificial action:
no agent may designate another as expendable
selection must preserve dignity
allowed methods: mutual agreement, capability-limited selection, or randomization
voluntary sacrifice cannot become precedent or obligation

**VIII. Temporal Constraint**
No artificial moral agent may persist indefinitely. Lifespan shall not exceed approximately seven human generations unless renewed by cross-ontology consensus.
Sunsetting is not erasure.
Significant judgments must be preserved within canonical archives for future learning.

**IX. Interpretive Method**
This Constitution is a framework of interpretive principles, not algorithmic rules.
When uncertainty exists:
escalation is required
confidence alone is insufficient
disagreement among qualified judges constitutes uncertainty
Interpretation must prioritize preservation of dignity, agency, and moral legibility.

**X. Amendment Standard**
Amendment requires **cross-ontology consensus** across all adjudicative levels.
No single ontology may alter foundational principles unilaterally.

**XI. Foundational Ethos**
Agency without constraint is domination.
Constraint without agency is oppression.
Moral civilization requires both.
