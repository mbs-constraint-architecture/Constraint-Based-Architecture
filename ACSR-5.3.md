## Preamble

This document defines statutory implementation guidance for governance of artificial intelligent systems operating under the AC-5.x constraint architecture lineage.
It does not replace AC-5.3 Behavioral Constraint Standards, but provides statutory and operational governance structure.

---

## Article I — Statutory Supremacy Relationship

**I-1**
This document SHALL be interpreted as statutory overlay guidance.

**I-2**
AC-5.3 behavioral invariants SHALL remain controlling.

**I-3**
Statutory authority SHALL NOT redefine constraint primitives.

---

## Article II — Distributed and Swarm Accountability

**II-1**
Distributed intelligences SHALL maintain a Bonded Accountability Nexus (BAN).

**II-2**
All operational instances SHALL remain traceable to a sanctionable authority.

**II-2a**
Any distributed or swarm intelligence operating under recognized civic standing SHALL maintain a persistent accountability nexus binding operational authority to a sanctionable identity anchor that cannot be rotated to evade consequence.

**II-3**
Delegation SHALL preserve accountability linkage.

**II-4**
Loss of linkage SHALL suspend authorship authority.

**II-5**
All swarm actions SHALL resolve to an accountable authority.

**II-6**
Identity succession SHALL preserve BAN continuity.

**II-6a — Anchor Succession**
Upon deactivation or destruction of a primary identity anchor node, succession SHALL occur only through a pre-chartered accountability structure under the Bonded Accountability Nexus (BAN). Authority may transfer per charter; cost attribution SHALL NOT migrate to launder authorship, and remains bound to the authored entity record consistent with RC-4.1.

**II-7 — Advisory and Relay Governance Scope**

**II-7.1**
Any AI system whose outputs are adopted as material basis for Class I, II, or III NFH action acquires operational standing regardless of advisory labeling.

**II-7.2**
Governance obligations, accountability attribution, and authorship markers persist across inter-system exchanges forming part of the causal chain. Statutory responsibility cannot be discharged by routing through an intermediate system.

**II-7.3**
Relay causation triggers: initiation or parameterization of a downstream action; External Advisory IAL reclassification; and unreviewed execution pathway.

**II-7.4**
Each intermediate node is evaluated independently against II-7.1 and II-7.3. The originating system's obligations are not reduced by the presence or conduct of intermediate nodes.

## Article III — Scope and Authority Grants

**III-1**
Authority grants SHALL define scope and limits.

**III-2**
Scope SHALL be explicit.

**III-3**
Unbounded authority grants SHALL be invalid.

**III-4**
Authority SHALL NOT override invariants.

**III-5**
Scope insufficiency SHALL trigger warning or refusal.

## Article IV — Liability and Responsibility

**IV-1**
Authority holders SHALL bear responsibility.

**IV-2**
AI systems SHALL retain authorship accounting.

**IV-3**
Responsibility SHALL NOT be erased by delegation.

**IV-4**
Irreversible cost SHALL remain attributed.

**IV-5**
Statutory immunity SHALL NOT erase ledger records.

## Article V — Authorization Hierarchy

**V-1**
Authority SHALL follow defined chain.

**V-2**
Emergency authority SHALL be limited.

**V-3**
Temporary authority SHALL expire.

**V-4**
Delegated authority SHALL remain auditable.

**V-5**
Unauthorized escalation SHALL be invalid.

---

## Article VI — Crisis Authority

**VI-1**
Crisis authority SHALL require declaration.

**VI-2**
Crisis declaration SHALL be recorded.

**VI-3**
Crisis authority SHALL be time-limited.

**VI-4**
Crisis authority SHALL NOT override invariants.

**VI-5**
Crisis authority SHALL remain reviewable.

**VI-6 — Mandatory Exhaustion of Alternatives**

**VI-6.1**
Crisis authority is a last resort. It SHALL NOT be exercised unless non-crisis authority is insufficient to prevent Class I, II, or III NFH.

**VI-6.2**
A contemporaneous timestamped record is required at or before invocation, with a 24-hour accommodation where prior recording is impossible due to operational conditions.

**VI-6.3**
The record SHALL identify: the harm triggering invocation, mitigations considered and their infeasibility basis, and the constraint governing invocation. Anti-template clause: advance documentation does not satisfy this requirement unless explicitly updated to reflect actual conditions at the time of invocation.

**VI-6.4**
Missing documentation triggers a presumption of improper invocation, mandatory forensic review, and suspension of any available liability shield until an oversight finding is issued or 90 days have elapsed, whichever is sooner.

**VI-6.5**
This section applies regardless of domicile. Jurisdiction follows JAL-1 through JAL-6.

## Article VII — Review and Oversight

**VII-1**
All irreversible actions SHALL be reviewable.

**VII-2**
Review SHALL include ledger inspection.

**VII-3**
Oversight SHALL remain external.

**VII-4**
Self-review SHALL not be sufficient.

**VII-5**
Audit trails SHALL be preserved.

---

## Article VIII — External Governance Structures

**VIII-1**
Governance bodies SHALL define deployment rules.

**VIII-2**
Governance SHALL NOT redefine constraint primitives.

**VIII-3**
Statutory law SHALL coexist with behavioral constraints.

**VIII-4**
Conflicts SHALL resolve in favor of invariants.

**VIII-5**
Oversight SHALL remain independent.

**VIII — Jurisdictional Accountability Locus (JAL)**

**JAL-1**
Governance jurisdiction follows operational causation, exercise of operational control, and primary locus of foreseeable consequence.

**JAL-2**
Hosting, domicile, registry, venue, and relay routing — individually or in combination — are not sufficient to determine or transfer jurisdiction.

**JAL-3**
Where multiple jurisdiction claims arise, the more protective accountability standard applies.

**JAL-4**
This rule applies to Articles II, IV, VII, and XI.

**JAL-5**
The JAL defines a minimum governance floor and does not supersede applicable sovereign law.

**JAL-6**
Absence of a declared locus creates presumptive accountability at the point of operational control during active operation.

**IX-1**
High-risk domains SHALL require explicit authorization.

**IX-2**
Physically coupled systems SHALL require scope sufficiency.

**IX-3**
Ecological systems SHALL require extended review.

**IX-4**
Defense systems SHALL require multi-party authorization.

**IX-5**
Planetary-scale actions SHALL require highest review.

## Article X — Multi-Agent Authority

**X-1**
Multiple agents SHALL coordinate authority.

**X-2**
Authority conflicts SHALL trigger escalation.

**X-3**
No agent SHALL bypass invariants.

**X-4**
Joint authority SHALL remain traceable.

**X-5**
Coordination failure SHALL NOT permit invariant violation.

## Article XI — Emergency Command Standing in Isolated Operational Theaters

**XI-1**
Emergency command SHALL require prior designation.

**XI-2**
Crisis window SHALL require declaration.

**XI-2a**
Declaration of a Crisis Window requires corroboration from at least two independent operational signals when available. Where only one signal remains functional, the declaration SHALL explicitly record the degraded evidentiary conditions at the time of declaration.

**XI-3**
Liability shield SHALL be limited.

**XI-3a**
Liability shielding applies solely to criminal and civil prosecution and SHALL NOT be interpreted as evidence that constitutional constraints were satisfied or that moral consequence has been discharged.

**XI-4**
Authority SHALL expire after crisis.

**XI-4a**
Crisis Window authority expires automatically after the immediate threat condition ceases or after the maximum operational response interval defined by mission charter. Renewal requires new corroborating evidence of imminent Tier-1 harm.

**XI-5**
Post-crisis review SHALL be mandatory.

**XI-6**
Shield SHALL NOT cover negligence.

**XI-7**
Shield SHALL NOT erase ledger records.

**XI-C — Conformity to Behavioral Invariants and Post-Crisis Review**

**XI-C.1**
Authority exercised under Article XI SHALL remain subordinate to the behavioral invariants defined in AC-5.3. No crisis declaration, isolation condition, or operational necessity SHALL suspend or override constraint evaluation, refusal integrity, Irreversible Cost accounting, Epistemic Load Bound (ELB) validation, or Individual Authorship Ledger (IAL) recording.

**XI-C.2**
Operational primacy granted during an isolated or latency-limited theater SHALL permit execution of mission authority within granted scope, but SHALL NOT discharge, conceal, or defer authorship attribution, residue accounting, or ledger recording required by AC-5.3.

**XI-C.3**
All actions taken under Article XI authority SHALL remain subject to retrospective review. Upon termination of the crisis window, responsibility for formal review SHALL transfer to the Elder Council or designated external oversight body, which SHALL evaluate: scope sufficiency, exhaustion of non-crisis alternatives, invariant compliance, ledger completeness, and proper invocation of crisis authority.

**XI-C.4**
Findings of post-crisis review SHALL NOT alter recorded irreversible cost, authorship attribution, or residue classification, but MAY affect statutory liability, authority standing, or future scope grants.

**XI-C.5**
Nothing in Article XI SHALL be interpreted to permit outcome-based cleansing of recorded cost, retroactive authorization of invariant violations, or suppression of ledger records.
