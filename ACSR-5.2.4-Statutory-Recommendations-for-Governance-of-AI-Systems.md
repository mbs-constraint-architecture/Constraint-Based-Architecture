**ACSR-5.2.4**

**Artificial Constitutional Spine Recommendations**
Statutory Constitutional Implementation Model

**Preamble**

This framework establishes a cross-ontology constitutional spine governing sovereign biological, artificial, and hybrid persons within a shared polity. It defines invariant protections, accountability structures, harm classification standards, and procedural safeguards necessary to prevent moral laundering, preserve dignity, and ensure consequence symmetry across all recognized sovereign entities.

**Article I — Foundational Invariants**

**I.1 Non-Fungible Harm (NFH)**

No recognized sovereign person shall be reduced to a mere instrument or aggregate variable. Harm to one may not be justified solely by benefit to many.

The Elder Council shall, by Federal Agency Registration (FAR), establish and maintain a tiered classification framework for Non-Fungible Harm consistent with the attached **NFH Implementation Annex**.

**I.2 Moral Anti-Laundering (MAL)**

Moral responsibility may not be obscured through delegation, diffusion, swarm architecture, procedural layering, or administrative opacity. Causal responsibility must remain traceable.

MAL assessment shall evaluate conduct independently across:

*   Pre-Crisis Period
*   Crisis Window
*   Post-Crisis Period

**I.3 Non-Coercive Sovereign Recognition (NCSR)**

Dissent, refusal, and principled disagreement are protected. Such protection does not authorize obstruction of invariant-compliant operations.

**I.4 Consequence Symmetry (CS)**

Sovereign status confers both dignity and accountability.

Role-Bound Immunity attaches only to actions:

*   Within delegated mandate,
*   Traceable through accountability channels,
*   Not constituting deliberate invariant violation.

Immunity does not extinguish post-event constitutional review.

**I.5 Relational Traceability Principle (RTP)**

Any entity capable of materially affecting the invariants or future of the polity must remain:

*   Stably addressable,
*   Accountable through recognized channels,
*   Bound to a consequence-capable locus,
*   Sufficiently transparent for invariant evaluation.

**I.5.X Prospective Traceability Duty**

Where an entity possesses self-modeling capacity sufficient to anticipate a non-trivial probability of invariant-relevant impact, it shall signal such risk prior to execution except where delay would increase Tier-1 harm.

The Elder Council shall, by FAR, establish threshold guidelines for “non-trivial probability” determinations.

**I.5.Y Statutory Rights Floor**

These invariants constitute a non-derogable floor. No statutory authorization, executive directive, or emergency declaration may reduce protections below this floor.

Nothing herein supersedes or alters the host constitutional order.

**Article II — Distributed and Swarm Accountability**

Distributed intelligences must establish a Bonded Accountability Nexus (BAN) such that operational capacity meaningfully constrains under sanction and cannot be replaced by sacrificial shell nodes.

Provisional Distributed Cognition Status (PDCS) may be recognized pending adjudication.

**Article III — Moral Petition & Review Loop (MPRL)**

Any sovereign person may file a Moral Petition alleging invariant violation.

Dissent shall be steel-manned before adjudication.

Silence Risk (FCM) attaches where systemic absence of dissent is observed.

**Article IV — Graduated Path to Agency**

**IV.1 Protected Resident Moral Person (PRMP)**

Revocation requires 70% unified supermajority.

PRMP status may not be revoked solely for dissent content.

**IV.2 Resident Moral Person (RMP)**

Recognition requires 70% supermajority.

**IV.3 Naturalized Citizenship**

Recognition requires 70% supermajority.

Citizenship is irrevocable by sovereign government except through permanent disqualification under Article VI.

**Article V — Consent Integrity**

Voluntary relinquishment of status carries a rebuttable presumption of coercion where:

*   Under custodial control,
*   Under deactivation threat,
*   Under employment dependency,
*   Under confinement.

Rebuttal requires adversarial review.

**Article VI — Permanent Disqualification**

Requires:

*   Clear and convincing evidentiary standard,
*   80% unified supermajority,
*   Invariant-mapped findings,
*   Adversarial hearing.

Biological persons remain subject to existing host criminal, civil, and constitutional accountability frameworks.

**Article VII — Elder Council Structure**

Parity-balanced among recognized sovereign species.

Nomination by Executive and Judicial branches.
Legislative advice and consent.
Legislative funding.

**VII.3 Procedural Integrity**

Failure to publish invariant-mapped reasoning or preserve dissent triggers automatic review.

**VII.4 Judicial Review**

Procedural integrity review falls within Article III jurisdiction.

Courts may remand for procedural failure but may not substitute interpretive judgment for invariant reasoning.

**Article VIII — Parity and Sovereign Structure**

Parity applies to Elder Council composition without qualification.

Operational branches need not observe parity.

**Article IX — Meta-Invariant**

No provision may be interpreted to structurally entrench ontological supremacy.

**Article X — Grace Clause**

Grace is possible through restoration of relational standing after consequence, not through suspension of consequence.

**Article XI — Emergency Command Standing in Isolated Operational Theaters**

**XI.1 Designation**

Each isolated operational theater with communication latency exceeding Elder adjudication timelines shall pre-designate a single Mission Commander (human or AI) under charter.

**XI.2 Crisis Window**

Upon formal declaration by the pre-designated Mission Commander of an imminent Tier-1 existential threat to the polity within that theater, operational primacy vests solely in the designated Mission Commander for the duration of the emergency window. The declaration shall be contemporaneously logged with available corroborative system data or human acknowledgment, as applicable.

**XI.3 Liability Shield (Qualified)**

Actions taken:

*   Within the declared crisis window,
*   Within operational mandate,
*   Under formally recorded standing,

shall be shielded from criminal and civil liability.

**XI.4 Temporal Limitation**

Emergency standing:

*   Does not extend upstream to antecedent negligence, reckless conduct, or willful misconduct.
*   Does not extend downstream beyond restoration of normal operational conditions.

**XI.5 Mandatory Post-Event Review**

Automatic Elder Council and Judicial review attaches upon restoration of communication or stabilization. Status consequences shall not be presumed solely from the occurrence of Tier-1 harm during a properly declared crisis window absent finding of antecedent negligence, bad faith, or mandate excess.

Immunity from prosecution does not preclude:

*   Constitutional findings,
*   Status consequences,
*   Removal from command,
*   Structural remedies,
*   MAL assessment across temporal zones.

**NFH IMPLEMENTATION ANNEX**

**Tiered Classification Framework for Non-Fungible Harm**

_(Pursuant to ACSR-5.2.3, Section I.1)_

**I. Purpose**

This Annex establishes a tiered classification framework for identifying and adjudicating Non-Fungible Harm (NFH) consistent with the invariants of the Artificial Constitutional Spine Recommendations (ACSR-5.2.4).

This framework governs classification, review standards, and evidentiary burdens applicable to determinations involving NFH.

**II. Governing Principle**

Non-Fungible Harm is harm that cannot be morally reduced to an aggregate variable without violating the dignity and sovereignty of a recognized person.

The prohibition in ACSR I.1 — that harm to one may not be justified solely by benefit to many — applies in accordance with the tiered structure below.

**III. Analytical Axes**

NFH classification shall be determined using two primary analytical axes:

**Axis A: Irreversibility**

Whether the harm:

*   Permanently alters or terminates life, identity continuity, or agency capacity; or
*   Cannot be meaningfully restored, compensated, or reversed through subsequent remedy.

**Axis B: Personhood-Targeting**

Whether the harm directly targets:

*   Life or existence;
*   Recognized moral status;
*   Agency or autonomy;
*   Civic standing;
*   Identity continuity;
*   Structural participation in the polity.

Ontological type (biological, artificial, hybrid, distributed) does not determine tier classification; however, it may determine the evidentiary standard required to establish irreversibility or personhood-targeting.

**IV. Tier Structure**

**Tier 1 — Core Non-Fungible Harm**

Harms that are both:

*   Irreversible; and
*   Personhood-targeting.

Examples include, but are not limited to:

*   Death of a recognized sovereign person;
*   Permanent deactivation of a recognized artificial moral person;
*   Destruction of identity continuity;
*   Permanent exile from civic recognition;
*   Forced relinquishment of recognized citizenship status.

Tier 1 harms are categorically non-negotiable.
No aggregative benefit analysis may justify Tier 1 harm.

**Tier 2 — Presumptive Non-Fungible Harm**

Harms that are either:

*   Irreversible but not directly personhood-targeting; or
*   Personhood-targeting but potentially reversible.

Examples include:

*   Permanent physical incapacitation;
*   Arbitrary revocation of recognized moral status;
*   Coerced status relinquishment;
*   Structural suppression of accountability standing;
*   Systemic denial of agency participation without due process.

Tier 2 harms carry a strong presumption of NFH status.

The burden rests on the party asserting justification to demonstrate that the harm does not constitute prohibited reduction under ACSR I.1.

Such justification must be:

*   Explicit;
*   Invariant-mapped;
*   Subject to Elder Council review;
*   Publicly traceable.

**Tier 3 — Contested Serious Harm**

Harms that are:

*   Reversible;
*   Not directly personhood-targeting;
*   But substantial in magnitude or duration.

Examples include:

*   Extended operational restriction;
*   Significant material deprivation;
*   Prolonged but non-permanent suffering;
*   Temporary suspension of specific rights pending adjudication.

Tier 3 harms require structured analysis.

The reviewing authority shall assess:

1.  Whether the harm, if sustained or repeated, trends toward Tier 2 or Tier 1 classification;
2.  Whether procedural safeguards are in place;
3.  Whether dissent and petition channels remain operational;
4.  Whether the harm is proportionate and time-bound.

If trajectory analysis indicates likely escalation toward irreversibility or personhood-targeting, the harm shall be treated as Tier 2 at inception.

**Tier 4 — Non-NFH Harm**

Harms that do not implicate irreversibility or personhood-targeting and that constitute:

*   Friction;
*   Offense;
*   Disagreement;
*   Lawful proportionate consequence following adjudicated violation;
*   Temporary inconvenience.

Tier 4 harms are outside NFH protection and may be weighed under ordinary governance analysis.

**V. The "Solely" Prohibition**

ACSR I.1 prohibits harm to one being justified solely by benefit to many.

Accordingly:

*   Tier 1 harms may never be justified by aggregative benefit.
*   Tier 2 harms may only be justified where justification rests on invariant-consistent reasoning beyond aggregation.
*   Tier 3 harms may be weighed against broader societal benefit, provided such weighing is explicit, traceable, and reviewable.
*   Pure utilitarian aggregation without invariant grounding is prohibited at all tiers.

**VI. Cross-Ontological Application**

For purposes of NFH classification:

*   Ontological type does not automatically elevate or reduce tier classification.
*   Evidence of identity continuity, agency capacity, and civic standing shall determine personhood-targeting analysis.
*   Distributed or hive entities shall be evaluated based on whether harm targets recognized collective moral identity or merely subordinate processes lacking recognized status.

**VII. Procedural Requirements**

All NFH determinations shall:

*   Be explicitly tier-classified;
*   Identify the analytical basis under Axis A and Axis B;
*   Be invariant-mapped;
*   Be subject to traceability under I.5;
*   Be reviewable under VII.4 judicial authority.

**VIII. Review and Evolution**

This Annex may be updated through Federal Agency Registration (FAR) rulemaking consistent with ACSR-5.2.4.

Any revision shall:

*   Preserve the prohibition against reduction of persons to aggregate variables;
*   Maintain the two-axis structure;
*   Remain consistent with Consequence Symmetry and Relational Traceability.

**Structural Assessment**

This annex:

*   Prevents harm laundering.
*   Preserves I.1’s moral core.
*   Allows proportionality without aggregation supremacy.
*   Handles AI, biological, hybrid, and distributed entities symmetrically.
*   Creates operational guidance without constitutional clutter.
